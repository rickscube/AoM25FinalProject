ANCIENT ERA

The Wandering Womb

The Belief: The uterus was an animal that roamed the body.

Reality: Anxiety, infection, or normal physiology.

Click to Inspect Case [+]

Archival Note: Scent Therapy

To treat this, physicians used "fumigation." They placed foul-smelling substances under a woman's nose to drive the uterus down, and sweet-smelling ones between her legs to attract it back to position.

This theory cemented "Hysteria" (from Greek hystera) as a medical validity for centuries.

MEDIEVAL ERA

The Corruption of Air

The Belief: "Miasma" (bad smells) caused the plague.

Reality: Bacteria (Y. pestis) on fleas.

Click to Inspect Case [+]

Archival Note: The Beak Mask

The iconic plague mask was functional design: the beak was stuffed with rose petals and camphor to filter the "bad air."

Ironically, while the theory was wrong, the focus on smell led to better sewage management, inadvertently reducing the rat population.

VICTORIAN ERA

Drapetomania

The Belief: The desire for freedom was a mental illness.

Reality: Scientific Racism.

Archival Note: Medicalizing Control

Dr. Samuel Cartwright defined "Drapetomania" as a disease of the mind causing enslaved people to flee. The prescribed treatment was "whipping the devil out of them."

Lesson: Science often bends to confirm existing power structures.

MODERNIST ERA

The Ice Pick Cure

The Belief: Severing the brain cures personality defects.

Reality: Permanent brain damage (Lobotomy).

Click to Inspect Case [+]

Archival Note: The Lobotomobile

Dr. Walter Freeman performed transorbital lobotomies (via the eye socket) in a traveling van. He won a Nobel Prize for the procedure in 1949.

It left thousands, including Rosemary Kennedy, incapacitated.

CONTEMPORARY

The "Yentl Syndrome"

The Belief: Heart attacks always look like crushing chest pain.

Reality: Female presentation differs (nausea, fatigue).

Click to Inspect Case [+]

Archival Note: The Male Default

Until 1993, women of childbearing age were excluded from clinical trials. Heart attack symptoms were defined by male biology.

Women presenting with nausea or jaw pain are often discharged with anti-anxiety meds while in cardiac arrest.
